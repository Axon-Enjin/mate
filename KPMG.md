# Guide

**ACADEMIC INNOVATION CHALLENGE \- AI AGENTS**  
**PRIMARY PLATFORM: COPILOT STUDIO**

**PROBLEM**  
University students often struggle to balance multiple syllabi, assignment deadlines, and personal study goals. Currently, students must manually read through course documents, extract due dates, and create reminders in their calendars or to-do lists. Because this process is tedious and fragmented, deadlines are frequently missed. Students often realize too late that several major projects are due in the same week, leading to increased stress and reduced academic performance.

**PROPOSED SOLUTION**  
Create an autonomous academic assistant that transforms a student’s static academic requirements into an active, adaptive study strategy. Rather than simply listing the tasks, the platform should orchestrate the student’s academic life.

The assistant should proactively identify deadline conflicts, suggest realistic study blocks based on student’s real-time availability, and act as an intelligent bridge between course materials and personal productivity tools. The agent should move beyond basic reminders and demonstrate reasoning about time, workload, and priorities.

**USER ROLES**  
Students (Primary Users)  
Students are responsible for:

1. Providing access to academic inputs such as syllabi, assignment briefs, and calendars  
2. Expressing goals or needs in lateral language (e.g., “Help me plan my week.”)  
3. Responding to clarification prompts when requests are incomplete or ambiguous  
4. Reviewing and approving suggested study schedules or adjustments  
5. Retaining responsibility for attending classes, submitting work, and meeting deadlines

Students are supported, not replaced, by the system’s recommendations.

**CRITERIA**  
50% \- Functionality and User Experience  
50% \- Data Accuracy & Relevance of Response

# **Mate**

# **AUTONOMOUS ACADEMIC ORCHESTRATOR**

# **Executive Summary**

Mate is an autonomous academic assistant developed on **Microsoft Copilot Studio** to address the systemic fragmentation of student workload management. By shifting from static reminders to an active, adaptive study strategy, Mate orchestrates a student’s academic life through proactive deadline conflict identification and intelligent reasoning about workload priorities.

# **Core Problem Statement**

University students frequently struggle with the manual extraction of data from disparate syllabi and assignment briefs, leading to missed deadlines and high-stress environments. Current tools are often passive; they require manual entry and fail to account for real-time availability or overlapping academic pressures.

# **Proposed Solution Architecture**

Mate acts as an intelligent bridge between course materials and productivity tools, transforming static requirements into dynamic action plans.

## **Key Features**

* **Syllabus Ingestion & Parsing:** Automatically extracts key dates and requirements from uploaded academic documents.  
* **Conflict Reasoning:** Proactively identifies weeks where multiple major projects are due and suggests early interventions.  
* **Adaptive Scheduling:** Suggests realistic study blocks based on the student's real-time availability and personal goals.  
* **Lateral Language Processing:** Interprets natural language requests such as "Help me plan my week" to generate comprehensive schedules.

# **Operational Framework**

The system operates on a collaborative model where the student retains final decision-making authority.

| User Responsibility | System Support |
| :---- | :---- |
| Providing access to syllabi and calendars | Extracting dates and creating a centralized dashboard |
| Expressing goals in natural language | Interpreting workload and suggesting priorities |
| Approving suggested schedules | Adjusting blocks based on feedback and conflicts |
| Attending classes and submitting work | Sending proactive nudges and reminders |
